# Brastel Remit Knowledge Base

This document serves as the primary source of truth for the Brastel Remit AI Assistant.

## 1. Overview
Brastel Remit is an international money transfer service from Japan. 
Official Website: https://brastelremit.jp/

## 2. Registration & KYC (Know Your Customer)
- **Required Documents**:
    - Valid Resident Card (Zairyu Card).
    - My Number (Individual Number) document (My Number Card or Juminhyo with My Number).
    - Optional: Driver's License (Japanese).
- **Process**:
    1. Download the Brastel Remit App.
    2. Upload photos of documents.
    3. Verification takes approximately **3 business days**.
- **Important**: Transfers can only start after the account is "Approved."

## 3. Transfer Fees (Japan to Overseas)
Fees are determined by the transfer amount:
- 1 JPY - 10,000 JPY: **480 JPY**
- 10,001 JPY - 30,000 JPY: **780 JPY**
- 30,001 JPY - 100,000 JPY: **1,280 JPY**
- 100,001 JPY - 800,000 JPY: **1,480 JPY**

*Note: The maximum limit per transaction is 800,000 JPY.*

## 4. Exchange Rates
Exchange rates are variable and updated frequently.
- **PHP (Philippines)**: ~0.38
- **VND (Vietnam)**: ~165.20
- **IDR (Indonesia)**: ~108.50
- **INR (India)**: ~0.55
- **NPR (Nepal)**: ~0.88

## 5. Deposit Methods
Before sending money, users must deposit JPY into their Brastel account:
- **Yucho (Japan Post) Deposit Card**: Use at any JP Bank ATM.
- **Lawson Bank ATM**: Using the Brastel card.
- **FamilyMart (FamiPort)**: Generate a code in the app and pay at the counter.
- **Bank Transfer (Furikomi)**: To Brastel's dedicated bank accounts.

## 7. Troubleshooting (Common Issues)
- **"Why is my transfer delayed?"**: Usually due to incomplete KYC or the recipient bank's processing time.
- **"I lost my deposit card"**: Contact support immediately to lock the card and request a replacement.
